Welcome to my website!
My name is Jonathan Ji, and I am a third-year undergrad studying Electrical Engineering & Computer Science at UC Berkeley. Who is broadly interested in the world of Computer Architecture and ASICs. 

I am currently taking a gap semester to work as an ASIC Design Verification Engineer Intern at AWS's Annapurna Labs, where I contribute to UVM-based verification of MLA IPs and build verification infrastructure. Outside of work, I am exploring the world of hardware and micro-architectural security research. 

Before, I worked on building fuzzing pipelines for formal verification tools under the supervision of Flavien Solt at the SLICE Lab at Berkeley. In my sophomore summer, I wrote embedded software for [Background Transient Observer on Nasa's COSI](https://cosi.ssl.berkeley.edu/bto/) satellite. And outside of research, I was the Electrical Lead for [CALSOL](https://calsol.berkeley.edu/), UC Berkeley's Solar Vehicle Racing Team. There, I learned how to survive integration hell and how to stay up for 28 hours without sleeping. 

Outside of the world of electronics, I enjoy playing soccer and playing the bass guitar!
